# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of **Delphinus**, pledge to foster
an open, respectful, collaborative, and professional environment for everyone involved
in the project.

Delphinus is intended to support software development through reusable extensions,
builders, binders, utilities, and related components. We are committed to making
participation in this project a harassment-free experience for all contributors,
regardless of background, experience, identity, role, or point of view.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming, respectful, and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints, experiences, and technical opinions
- Gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the project, its users, and its maintainability
- Communicating with professionalism in issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions
- Helping others learn, contribute, and improve the project safely

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or offensive comments
- Personal attacks, insults, or demeaning language
- Trolling, inflammatory remarks, or deliberate disruption of project activities
- Publishing another person’s private information without explicit permission
- Repeatedly disregarding maintainer guidance, review standards, or project boundaries
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered unprofessional in a workplace or open source community

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Delphinus project spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- Issues, pull requests, discussions, and code reviews
- Documentation, examples, and release notes
- Community communication channels
- Public or private interactions made in the context of the project

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or the community.
Examples of representing the project include using an official project email address,
posting through an official social media account, or acting as an appointed maintainer.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of
acceptable behavior.

They have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits,
code, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that are not aligned
with this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently limit participation when behavior is
considered inappropriate, harmful, or disruptive to the project or its community.
Repeated or severe violations may result in temporary or permanent exclusion from the project.

---

## Reporting Issues

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project
maintainers through a private and appropriate communication channel.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly, fairly, and confidentially.
Maintainers are expected to respect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports an issue.
